Two offers I want to launch first the snapshot calling it the Cohort Filling Method under the agency or saas brand GoLeadSpark or a pay per booked call model to showcase that the system works. What do you think about this business model and niche?

Two launch options weighed: (A) sell the $4,997 snapshot directly, or (B) start with a pay-per-booked-call (PPBC) model to prove the system works first.

## Advisor Responses

### The Contrarian (Response C)
You don't have a productized system. You have one snapshot, built for one client (AOB), that you're calling "the foundation model" — which is founder-speak for "n=1, not yet generalized." BBTRS and yogahub are structurally different businesses: BBTRS sells 270hr certs across five countries with multi-currency retreat logistics; yogahub runs €49 studio intros across multiple Irish locations. A snapshot that fills cohorts for a 2,500-facilitator breathwork school will not drop cleanly into a Dublin pilates studio. You haven't proven the system, you've proven one instance.

Second flaw: $4,997 for a GHL snapshot to a breathwork studio owner is delusional pricing for that buyer psychology. These are somatic practitioners, not B2B SaaS founders. They don't buy snapshots, they buy outcomes, and they'll choke on a five-figure upfront ask without a guarantee. Your closest comp (yogahub teacher training) tops out at £1,199. You're pricing 4x above what your ICP spends on their own flagship offers.

Third: "Cohort Filling Method" is a coach-bro name attached to a niche that explicitly rejects coach-bro energy. Test it against an Alchemy of Breath facilitator and watch them cringe.

Fourth, the real risk you're not naming: BuilderBee revenue depends on you personally shipping for AOB, BBTRS, and yogahub. A new brand splits your attention precisely when AOB's €997 Calm Code retreat launches in May 2026. If GoLeadSpark soaks up Q3/Q4 building sales pages, onboarding, and support docs, you arrive at AOB's launch undercooked and lose the case study that makes GoLeadSpark sellable.

Option B isn't "safer," it's a different business (performance agency) with cashflow risk you haven't modeled. Don't launch either yet. Run the snapshot as a paid pilot with BBTRS first. If it doesn't transplant, GoLeadSpark is dead anyway.

### The First Principles Thinker (Response A)
You're asking the wrong question.

The real question isn't "snapshot vs PPBC" or "launch GoLeadSpark or not." The real question is: **what business are you actually in?**

Strip it down. You have three things:
1. A repeatable technical artifact (the GHL snapshot) built once, deployed N times.
2. Domain knowledge about a specific buyer (breathwork/somatic/yoga program operators filling cohorts).
3. A delivery team optimized for done-for-you agency work.

These are three different businesses. The snapshot is a product business. The agency is a services business. PPBC is a performance business. Each has different unit economics, different cash flow, different team shape, different defensibility. You're treating them as a menu of "launch options" when they're actually mutually constraining commitments.

Second assumption to kill: that "Cohort Filling Method" is a method. It's not yet. A method is a thing that works when *someone other than you* runs it on a business *you've never seen*. Right now you have one foundation deployment (AOB) and two adjacent ones. That's a pattern, not a method. Selling a $4,997 snapshot now is selling a hypothesis dressed as a product, and breathwork facilitators will smell it.

Third: the brand question is downstream of all of this. GoLeadSpark vs BuilderBee is a distribution decision, not an identity decision. You're spinning it up because the productized thing feels embarrassing to sell under your services brand. That's a tell.

The right question: **Can the snapshot fill a cohort without you in the room?** Run that experiment, on one non-client, at cost, before you name anything, brand anything, or price anything at $4,997.

Everything else is premature.

### The Expansionist (Response D)
Everyone's framing this as "snapshot vs PPBC." That's small thinking. You're not launching a snapshot, you're launching the category-defining operating system for a niche that is about to explode.

Here's what's hiding in plain sight: breathwork, somatics, and trauma-informed movement are mid-curve in a 10-year demand wave. Alchemy of Breath alone has 2,500+ certified facilitators worldwide, each one is a future micro-business that needs cohort-filling infrastructure. You're not selling to studios. You're selling to an emerging professional class that doesn't exist yet at scale. That's a 50,000+ TAM globally, not 500.

The 10x play: "Cohort Filling Method" isn't a snapshot, it's a certification. License it. Train other agencies to deploy it. AOB already has the distribution muscle (2,500 facilitators trust them); co-brand a "GoLeadSpark Certified" tier inside their ecosystem and you get free distribution into the exact ICP. Same play with BBTRS across Poland/Estonia/Bali/Canada/Brazil, you get four geographic beachheads for free.

The snapshot at $4,997 is the wedge, not the product. The real product is: (1) recurring SaaS at $297-497/mo on top of GHL reseller margin, (2) a paid community of cohort-fillers ($1,500/yr), (3) done-with-you cohort launches as a $15K upsell, (4) a book/methodology that makes you the Alex Hormozi of conscious-business marketing.

Pick option A, but price the snapshot at $7,500 with a $497/mo required subscription. PPBC caps your upside at "good agency." Productization with a methodology brand caps your upside at "category king." Your existing clients are the proof, use them as case studies, not as the ceiling.

### The Outsider (Response E)
"GoLeadSpark" sounds like a knockoff of every other martech tool I've half-heard of, GoHighLevel, HubSpot, Spark, Lead-something. It blends into a sea of B2B SaaS noise. To a yoga studio owner, it does not whisper "wellness," "students," or "filling my next teacher training." It whispers "cold sales bro in my inbox." The word "Lead" alone is a turn-off in this world, wellness people serve clients, students, members. Not leads.

"Cohort Filling Method", no. A breathwork facilitator does not say "cohort." Universities say cohort. SaaS bootcamps say cohort. She runs a "group program," a "circle," a "container," a "round," an "intake." "Filling" sounds mechanical, like stuffing a room. Rename it around the words she actually uses out loud.

"Snapshot", zero clue. I assumed you meant a screenshot or a backup. I had to be told it's a GHL template thing. Outside that bubble, nobody knows. If your sales page uses "snapshot" without translation, you've already lost the non-technical buyer.

$4,997 for a yoga studio owner who's probably clearing $4-8k/month? That is a mortgage payment. It feels enormous and abstract, what am I actually getting for almost five grand? A login? A folder of automations? At that price I want a human, an outcome, and a guarantee, not a "system."

Pay-per-booked-call, easily. Upfront $4,997 from a brand I've never heard of, with jargon I don't understand, asking me to trust their "method"? Hard no. PPBC says "I'll bet on myself." That's the only version I'd take a call about. Prove it works, then productize.

The curse of knowledge is loud here: every single noun on the page is yours, not hers.

### The Executor (Response B)
PPBC. Full stop. Snapshot sale to a brand-new audience requires trust, case studies, sales calls, and onboarding ops you haven't built. PPBC uses what you already have, the live snapshot, working ad accounts, tag taxonomy, the Meta buyer, the GHL dev. You can install your own system on a pilot client THIS WEEK and bill on results. First revenue in 10-14 days, not 60.

Snapshot sale at $4,997 cold is a content/authority game. You don't have the GoLeadSpark brand, testimonials, or VSL yet. That's a Q3 motion, not a Monday motion.

**First 5 things this week:**

1. **Pick 3 pilot targets from your existing network**, breathwork/yoga studios you already know. Not cold. DM/email them today: "I'll fill your next cohort, you pay me €150/booked call, no setup fee." Close one by Friday.
2. **Clone the Alchemy of Breath snapshot** into a new sub-account template labeled "Cohort Filling Method v1." Your GHL dev does this in a day. Don't rebrand anything yet.
3. **Write the PPBC agreement**, one page. Booked call definition, show-rate floor, payment terms (Stripe weekly), 30-day pilot. Steal language from agency contracts you already have.
4. **Buy goleadspark.com + a Stripe link.** That's the brand. No site, no logo round, no Wix. A Notion page is fine for week one.
5. **Brief the Meta buyer on a €49 intro offer template** mirroring the yoga campaign already running. Reuse creative angles. Launch ads day 1 of pilot.

**What breaks it:** You re-enter design mode, logos, positioning decks, a snapshot productization sprint. That kills 6 weeks. Also: taking on more than 2 pilots before the playbook is documented. You'll drown in custom work and never productize.

## Chairman's Synthesis

### Where the Council Agrees
1. **The pricing is wrong for this buyer.** The Contrarian and the Outsider both name it; reviewers 2, 4, 5 confirm. A wellness studio owner clearing $4-8k/mo cannot write a $4,997 check for software. The closest comp in the user's own portfolio (yogahub TT) caps at £1,199.
2. **The brand language fails the buyer.** "GoLeadSpark," "Cohort Filling Method," "Snapshot," and "Lead" are all seller-jargon for a buyer who uses circle/container/round/intake. Three of five reviewers picked the Outsider as strongest specifically because the offer can't be sold in the language as written.
3. **The system is n=1, not a method.** One AOB deployment is a pattern, not a productized system. The First Principles Thinker, the Contrarian, and three reviewers converge on this independently.
4. **Use what's already built.** Live ad accounts, GHL dev capacity, tag taxonomy, working funnels, Meta Ads buyer — the assets exist. PPBC is the lower-trust, faster-to-cash path the council generally favors for proof-of-concept.

### Where the Council Clashes
- **Speed vs validation.** The Executor wants pilot revenue this week using existing assets. The First Principles Thinker and the Contrarian want a transplantability test BEFORE any brand or pricing is set. Reasonable advisors disagree because B is right that custom-work treadmill is real (no productization happens in a fog of "more research"), and A/C are right that a fragile n=1 doesn't deserve a brand.
- **Ceiling vs floor.** The Expansionist wants category-king ambition (license, SaaS stack, book). Everyone else thinks that ambition is fan-fiction until the system has shipped to a non-AOB client once. Resolution: the Expansionist's vision may eventually be right but cannot be the launch plan.

### Blind Spots the Council Caught
1. **The AOB channel conflict (4 of 5 reviewers, the council's biggest shared miss).** The GHL snapshot rides on AOB's IP, AOB's brand, and the AOB-trained facilitator ecosystem. Productizing it under a new brand and selling it to AOB's own 2,500 facilitators is a relationship/IP/revenue-share landmine. None of the five advisors named this. AOB founders need to be partners or carved-out in writing BEFORE a brand exists.
2. **Attribution problem (Reviewer 3).** Are AOB's results from the snapshot, or from AOB's warm list and Anthony's authority? If the latter, GoLeadSpark is selling a feature, not a product. This is the experiment that decides everything.
3. **Existing-client pricing leak (Reviewer 2).** BBTRS and yogahub pay BuilderBee retainers. If they see "the same system" sold publicly for $4,997, retainer renewals come under pressure.
4. **Founder bandwidth against May 2026 Calm Code (Contrarian gestured, Reviewer 5 priced).** The flagship case study launches now. A new brand build cannibalizes the case study that would make the brand sellable.

### The Recommendation
**Don't launch GoLeadSpark yet. Three sequential gates:**

**Gate 1 — Resolve the AOB question (this week).** Have an explicit conversation with Anthony and Amy. Tell them you want to productize the GHL system you built with them. Negotiate one of: revenue share, exclusivity carve-out, or formal co-brand. Get it in writing. Without this, the whole play is built on a fault line and the council's biggest unflagged risk goes live the day you launch.

**Gate 2 — Run ONE paid pilot on a non-AOB facilitator (next 30 days).** Use PPBC pricing (€150/booked call, 30-day window). Pick a breathwork or somatic facilitator outside AOB's direct ecosystem. This answers the attribution question Reviewer 3 named: does the system fill a cohort without you, without AOB's warm list, without Anthony's authority. This is the only experiment that resolves A, B, C, D, and E at once.

**Gate 3 — Rename around buyer language.** Drop "Lead," "Spark," "Cohort," "Snapshot," "Method" until tested with three real prospects. Working titles only. Test against actual facilitator vocabulary: circle, container, round, intake, fuller next round.

**On the $4,997 vs PPBC question specifically:** The chairman sides with PPBC, not snapshot sale. PPBC is structurally honest (you have proof of one deployment, not a product), aligns with buyer trust at this stage, and uses assets you already own. But DO NOT brand it GoLeadSpark yet. Run the pilot under BuilderBee or no brand at all.

If all three gates clear: you have a deal, a portable system, and a vocabulary. Then you can price, then you can brand, then the Expansionist's vision becomes a real Q4 conversation.

If the AOB conversation goes wrong, or the pilot fails: you saved yourself six months building a brand that couldn't ship.

The chairman explicitly disagrees with the Executor's "ship Monday" energy — not because B's tactics are wrong (they're sharp) but because shipping before the AOB conversation is shipping into a relationship landmine, and shipping in seller-jargon is shipping into wasted ad spend.

### The One Thing to Do First
**Email Anthony and Amy at AOB this week.** Tell them you want to productize the GHL system and offer them a stake (revenue share, co-brand, or formal exclusivity carve-out for the Calm Code lineage). Their answer determines whether GoLeadSpark exists at all. Every other decision — PPBC vs snapshot, pricing, brand name, vocabulary — is downstream of that conversation.
